Design Statement

This site presents diabetes-friendly recipes and practical advice in a clear, accessible layout. It was built to meet the project's rubric requirements for design, layout, navigation, mechanics, and credibility.

Why these choices?

  • Color palette: Greens and pinks convey health, calm, and approachability while remaining readable (green: growth/health; pink: warmth/support).
  • Typography & fluid type scale: A readable sans-serif base (local fonts from Google Webfonts Helper) with a fluid `clamp()` scale ensures text is comfortable on all devices.
  • Layout: Grid and flex layouts create uncluttered pages and responsive cards for recipes — easy to scan and navigate.
  • Navigation: A consistent, top-level nav appears on all pages with clear, meaningful titles per the rubric.
  • Credibility: Contact and author information appear in footer/contact page; content is labeled educational, not medical advice.

How this meets the rubric

  1. Design: Visually pleasing and appropriate for the target audience (people seeking blood-sugar-friendly meals).
  2. Layout: Clean container, readable palettes, and effective white space for legibility.
  3. Navigation: Consistent nav on each page, descriptive page titles, relevant link labels.
  4. Mechanics: No broken links (replace placeholder images if needed), consistent layout across pages.
  5. Credibility: Contact information is included; author name is clearly displayed.
